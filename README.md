## Article Review

### [Genie 3: A new frontier for world models](https://deepmind.google/blog/genie-3-a-new-frontier-for-world-models/)

What I found interesting about the Genie 3 article is how it frames world models not just as an AI research concept, but as a large-scale engineered system. Rather than focusing on individual model performance, the article emphasizes building a coherent, interactive simulation of environments, which highlights challenges such as scalability, system integration, and long-term consistency. This perspective makes it clear that advances like Genie 3 are as much about software engineering and system design as they are about machine learning.

I also found it compelling that this kind of model could change how software is developed in the future, especially in areas like game development and simulation-based testing. If environments can be generated and interacted with dynamically, developers may prototype, test, and iterate on software in fundamentally new ways. Even though the article is not a coding tutorial, it illustrates how complex software systems emerge from the combination of research ideas and engineering decisions, which makes it indirectly but clearly related to software development and engineering.
